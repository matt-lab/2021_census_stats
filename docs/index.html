Australian’s age and sex

Data from the 2021 national census

Author

Matthew Andreotta

Published

February 3, 2023

This webpage shares results from the the 2021 Australian census on age, sex and income. This data may be useful for building survey samples representative of Australians. In particular: Table 1 describes the proportion of Australians across age categories; Table 2 describes the proportion of Australians across sexes; and Table 4 describes the annual household income of Australians.

Raw count data is provided in Table 5 and Table 6.

Introduction

In 2021, the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) conducted a census of Australians. Methodology can be found on the ABS’ website. For age, respondents were asked their date of birth or age. For sex, respondents were asked to select whether the person was: “male”, “female”, or “non-binary sex”.1 For income, respondents were asked to report their total weekly income. To calculate household income, the total weekly income was summed for each resident aged 15 years or older present in the household on Census Night.2 In this document, I have used the same terminology as the census.

Footnotes

  1. A word of warning. As per ABS’ own words:

    The non-binary sex option was marked on the Census form for 43,220 respondents or 0.17% of the Australian population. Analysis indicates this number is not indicative of any single characteristic. Responses show the concept of non-binary sex was not consistently understood and was perceived in different ways by different people. Results cannot be used as a measure of gender diversity, non-binary genders or trans populations. Additionally, it cannot be used as a measure for diverse sexualities, nor can it be interpreted as the number of people with variations of sex characteristics.

    For these reasons and more, I have ignored non-binary sex responses when presenting sex statistics.

    I believe the age data used for the statistics in this document do not include respondents who selected the non-binary sex response.↩︎
